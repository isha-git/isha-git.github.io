Background

Thank you for taking an interest in my profile.

Welcome to my webpage! My journey began during my undergraduate studies, where I became fascinated with neural networks and their potential. Since then, I have worked on several projects and research internships, including stock data forecasting, pollution estimation, and disease susceptibility prediction. My undergraduate thesis focused on developing image denoising techniques using a shallow convolutional neural network, which outperformed state-of-the-art algorithms by identifying regions of the input image that suggested noise.

I worked as an AI Research Engineer for Splink Inc., a Japanese start-up, on Alzheimer's Disease prediction using neuroimages. Recently, I completed my Master's in computer science with Artificial Intelligence track at Delft University of Technology. For my graduate thesis, I analyzed millions of microbiome DNA sequences and studied their association with colorectal cancer and type-2 diabetes using self-supervised learning.

My passion for data analysis extends beyond specific projects or research topics. I believe in understanding the "why" behind machine learning models' predictions, emphasizing feature importance and model interpretability. I am actively seeking new opportunities in machine learning and am excited to continue exploring the vast potential of this exciting field.
